Mapping Health Data

This project maps racial population percentages and infant mortality rates by county across South Carolina using U.S. Census and SC DHEC data.

Race and Infant Mortality

The data reveals significant correlations between racial demographics and infant mortality rates in South Carolina. Counties with higher percentages of Black or African American populations tend to show elevated infant mortality rates, highlighting persistent health disparities. This pattern suggests the need for targeted healthcare interventions and resources in these communities.

Tools Used

ArcGIS Dot Density Mapping Choropleth Mapping Inset Map Design Table Joins Legend Conversion

Key Insights

  • Racial percentages vary significantly by county, particularly for Black or African American populations.
  • Infant mortality rates are elevated in several rural and racialized counties.
  • Dot density maps provide spatial intuition for total population.
  • Choropleth maps reveal normalized disparities more effectively.
